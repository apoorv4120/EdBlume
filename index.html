About EdBlume

EdBlume Research Foundation is a registered nonprofit (Section 8, Companies Act, 2013) focused on promoting emotional well-being, empowering educators, and supporting neurodiverse learners through research, training, and inclusive programs. It was founded to take knowledge beyond classrooms and create real-world impact. By bringing together educators, researchers, students, policymakers, and communities, the foundation aims to raise educational standards, foster equity, and strengthen the social fabric. At its core, EdBlume is about turning research into action and using education as a force for lasting, positive change.

Mission

To advance educational research and social welfare by bringing together communities, educators, and researchers, creating practical solutions that promote equity, improve learning, and drive positive, lasting change for all.

Our Framework

The EdBlume Framework is a research-driven model built around three foundational intersections:

01
Research + Collaboration → Innovation We combine evidence-based inquiry with input from educators, institutions, and parents to co-create scalable, real-world solutions.
02
Training + Collaboration → Synergy Our programs are designed with stakeholders at the center—ensuring collective ownership and long-term success.
03
Research + Training → Empowerment By translating research into accessible, inclusive training, we equip educators, parents, and learners to make informed, confident decisions.

What We Do

01
EmLab – Emotional Ecology in Action
A research program based on Bronfenbrenner's Ecological Systems Theory and inspired by emotion studies by Hong & Cross, EmLab explores how emotional well-being is shaped by our environments.
Contact For More Info

What We Offer

  • Thematic survey-based research
  • Emotional Intelligence Index (EII)
  • Application of the EmEco model
  • Personalized reports for institutions

Who We Work With

  • Students, Teachers, Corporate Employees
  • Parents and Educational Institutions
  • Research Organizations

Outcomes

Emotionally self-aware individuals equipped to thrive in academic, personal, and professional spaces.

02
NeuroNest – For Neurodiverse Minds
A structured homeschooling and after-school support program for children with Autism and ADHD, built to complement—not replace—mainstream education.
Contact For More Info

What We Offer

  • Skill-building modules: 3, 6, and 12-month durations
  • Executive functioning (organization, focus)
  • Emotional regulation and communication
  • Life skills and self-advocacy
  • Confidence-building in real-world settings

Who We Support

  • Neurodiverse children and their families
  • Parents seeking specialized support
  • Educators working with neurodiverse students

Outcomes

Improved social, emotional, and cognitive functioning for long-term independence and classroom integration.

03
EdBlume Teachers – Empowering Women Educators
A 6-month intensive online teacher training program designed for women returning to work, career changers, and those with a passion for teaching but no formal classroom experience.
Contact For More Info

What We Offer

  • Live online classes three times a week
  • Assessments and reflective teaching practice
  • Emotional intelligence and pedagogy modules

Who We Work With

  • Women with basic qualifications but limited classroom experience

A pool of confident, skilled, certified women educators ready to fill critical staffing gaps without compromising on quality.

Capacity Building

Neurodiverse Support
Offer tailored programs for children with ASD and ADHD. Facilitate smoother integration into mainstream education.

Our Story

We started EdBlume with a single, powerful realization: something essential was missing in our schools—the emotional well-being of those who live and learn within them. After decades in classrooms and leadership roles, we witnessed the quiet cracks in the system.

Teachers overwhelmed by deadlines. Substitution teachers burdened with extra syllabus coverage when regular teachers were on leave. Neurodiverse learners struggling to keep pace in classrooms not built for their needs. Parents feeling helpless. Teachers silently exhausted. And students—especially those who needed support the most—falling further behind. Not out of negligence, but because the system kept moving, too fast to stop and care.

EdBlume was born to address these challenges, to slow things down and bring human values back into education, to support those who teach, uplift those who learn differently, and remind everyone that no child should be left behind—not because of a system's pace, but in spite of it. Because when we nurture the emotional lives of teachers and students, we don't just improve learning….we transform lives.

Meet Our Team

Passionate professionals driving educational transformation

Dr. Indrani Singh

Founder & CEO

Dr. Indrani Singh is an educator, strategic leader, and social impact advocate with over 30 years of experience in teaching, institutional leadership, and educational research.

Dr. Singh is academic strategist, transformational leader, and a mixed methods researcher with over three decades of experience spanning K 12 schools, and higher education. Holding a Ph.D. from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, she brings academic rigor and a global perspective to her work.

As a certified TBE (Transforming By Excellence) Assessor and Trainer, Dr. Singh specializes in academic excellence, institutional growth, and building emotionally resilient learning environments. Her Emotional Ecology framework explores the emotional and ecological factors influencing student success by bridging empathy with data-driven strategies.

She previously served as Vice President of Institutional and Undergraduate Research at Saint Augustine's University, where she led major initiatives in strategic planning, accreditation, and data-informed decision-making.
